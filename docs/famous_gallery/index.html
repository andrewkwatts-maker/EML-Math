EML-Math — Famous equations

Each equation is parsed from a single EML: … string and rendered client-side as a top-down flow diagram. Inputs are at the top, the output sits at the bottom; every junction is the binary EML primitive eml(L, R) = exp(L) − ln(R).

Physics

Einstein mass-energy equivalence E = m c²

Inputs: m, c   Output: E

Newton's second law F = m a

Inputs: m, a   Output: F

Newton's law of universal gravitation F = G M m / r²

Inputs: G, M, m, r   Output: F

Coulomb's law F = k q₁ q₂ / r²

Inputs: k, q1, q2, r   Output: F

Planck-Einstein relation E = h f

Inputs: h, f   Output: E

de Broglie wavelength λ = h / p

Inputs: h, p   Output: lambda

Stefan-Boltzmann law P = σ T⁴

Inputs: sigma, T   Output: P

Lorentz factor γ = 1 / √(1 − v²/c²)

Inputs: v, c   Output: gamma

Relativistic energy-momentum relation E = √((pc)² + (mc²)²)

Inputs: p, m, c   Output: E

Classical kinetic energy KE = ½ m v²

Inputs: m, v   Output: KE

Ohm's law V = I R

Inputs: I, R   Output: V

Ideal gas law (solved for P) P = n R T / V

Inputs: n, R, T, V   Output: P

Geometry

Pythagorean theorem c = √(a² + b²)

Inputs: a, b   Output: c

Area of a circle A = π r²

Inputs: r   Output: A

π enters via eml_pi() rather than a 3.14159… literal so the diagram shows it as a named symbol.

Circumference of a circle C = 2π r

Inputs: r   Output: C

Inputs: r   Output: V

Surface area of a sphere A = 4π r²

Inputs: r   Output: A

Volume of a cone V = (1/3) π r² h

Inputs: r, h   Output: V

Euclidean distance (2D) d = √((x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²)

Inputs: x1, y1, x2, y2   Output: d

Math

Quadratic formula x = (−b + sign·√(b² − 4ac)) / 2a

Inputs: a, b, c, sign   Output: x

EML is real-valued, so the ± of the textbook quadratic formula is encoded as an explicit `sign` input ∈ {-1, +1}. Evaluating with sign=+1 gives one root, sign=-1 the other. (Complex numbers are avoided because they would break the single-real-axis EML primitive.)

Golden ratio φ = (1 + √5) / 2

Inputs: (none)   Output: phi

Basel-problem term (single n) aₙ = 1 / n²

Inputs: n   Output: a_n

The Basel sum Σ 1/n² = π²/6 — the term shown is the summand.

Compound interest A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Inputs: P, r, n, t   Output: A

Inputs: x   Output: f
